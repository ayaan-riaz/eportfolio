Black Box Investigation

By: Ayaan Riaz

Introduction/Background Information

The mystery Black Box a creation of Mr. Sinusas. The inside of the box is unknown but with tests I will get a basic idea of what the inside is. You pour water in the top of the funnel (input) and water comes out of the bottom hole/pipe (output). I am trying to replicate the Mystery Black Box and will build a real life model and 3d model in maya as well.

Claim

I think that the Black Box contains a tipping bucket mechanism. The water will enter from a funnel and be poured into a bucket. As the bucket fills it will rotate and pour the water into a separate container from which the water will drain and exit the box via a tube. This would mimic the Mystery Black Box’s randomness in the amount of water exiting the box.

Evidence

The tipping bucket mechanism contains a bucket with a rod/axle on one side. The bucket also has a counterweight on that side where the axle is so it stays in an upright position. As the bucket fills with water, the weight causes it to tip and release the water into another container from which the water will be drained out of the black box model via a tube. We collected data from the Black Box by adding a measured amount of water and measuring how much of that water exited the Black Box. When we created our model we got a box, two buckets, two hoses different in size, one smaller than the other, two funnels, an axle/bamboo stick, and some counterweight. We used tools like scissors, tape, and a drill. We attached a hose/pipe to the funnel and inserted it in a box. Then we got a bucket with the axle and counterweight attached and poked it through the box so it could swing with the weight of water. We then had another bucket under the swinging bucket so it could catch the water and a hole to lead it into a funnel which had another hose attached to it so the water could then exit the box. We also noticed that the black box made a water pouring noise and so did ours so we were on the right track. For example in one of our trials we poured in 1,000 mL of water and none came out but then in another trial we added around 500 mL of water and a whole bunch came out. It was more than we added as if the water from the first and second pour were combined. Similar to the Mystery Black Box.

Reasoning

Reasoning Part I

We are using a tilting mechanism so the amount of water exiting the bucket will be different than the input water. This is because the bucket will need to be filled first and then would tip due to the weight of water. However, as the water gets released the bucket will return to its original position. There will still be water in the bucket meaning less water exited the black box than the amount that entered. To add more randomness, we were thinking of adding a mechanism which would make the bucket slowly return to the original position so that it could allow water to flow through differently every test.

Reasoning Part II

Our evidence supports our claim because our model performed closely to the black box. However it was different because of the size and the fact we weren’t able to attach hydraulics or springs with the limited materials and resources provided.

Reasoning Part III

The data would be different if the mechanism was a ball which lifts up a flap as the water level goes up to release the water because then it would release the same amount of water with only a little bit of change between tests. It would also leak a lot of water which was noticed by the Mystery Black Box but the Mystery Black Box did not leak as much water as that idea would. The Mystery Black Box also released the water gradually while that idea would release it suddenly.
